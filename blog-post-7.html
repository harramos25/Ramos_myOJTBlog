January 20–23, 2026

Technical Work and Event Participation

January 20, 2026 — Half-Day Duty Due to Health Condition

On January 20, I informed Ma’am Nor that I was not feeling well and requested permission to render only a half-day duty. Despite my condition, I still reported to the office and completed the tasks that I was able to handle within the limited time.

During my half-day duty, I made sure to stay updated with ongoing tasks and maintained communication regarding my responsibilities. This experience reminded me of the importance of being honest and professional when health concerns arise, while still showing commitment to work whenever possible.

January 21, 2026 — Project Development and Event Preparation

On this day, I continued working on the coding tasks related to the NCIP Job Portal during the morning hours. I focused on improving the system and applying corrections to ensure better functionality and structure.

In the afternoon, all interns were instructed to help clean and prepare the conference room in anticipation of upcoming visitors. We assisted in organizing the area and decorating the venue for the scheduled event. Additionally, Sir Mat assigned me to assist him as a photographer for the following day’s event, which allowed me to prepare and familiarize myself with the role.

This day highlighted the importance of teamwork and flexibility in handling both technical and non-technical responsibilities.

January 22, 2026 — Event Documentation and Photography Assistance

On January 22, Hadjara and I were assigned as photographers during the event titled “Turn-over Ceremony of Sulu Province to NCIP Region IX by MIPA-BARMM.” Our task was to document the event by capturing important moments, participants, and proceedings.

This experience allowed me to assist in a role beyond my usual technical tasks. It helped enhance my awareness of event documentation, coordination, and attention to detail. Being trusted with this responsibility showed the importance of adaptability and reliability in a professional environment.

The day was busy but rewarding, as it provided exposure to official events and administrative functions within the organization.

January 23, 2026 — Assembly Participation and Regular Office Duty

In the morning, we attended the Graduating Student General Assembly and Batch Officers Election. This activity allowed us to participate in an organized gathering that focused on student coordination and leadership.

After the assembly, we returned to the office in the afternoon to continue our regular duties. I resumed my assigned tasks and assisted with ongoing work in the office. The day was balanced between participation in formal activities and completion of routine responsibilities.

This experience reinforced the importance of being engaged in both organizational events and daily office work.
